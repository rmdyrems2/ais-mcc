ACCOUNTING INFORMATION SYSTEM

Definition of terms

For a better understanding of the study, the following terms below are defined.

Accounting Information Management System (AIMS) – It is a computer system that collects data and aggregates it into information that supports management decision making. The baseline system typically contains a prepackaged set of reports that are constantly updated, and which authorized users can access at any time. A more advanced system includes a query capability that allows users to create their own reports and conduct “what if” simulations. An accounting management information system can be used to address specific problems that crop up within an organization, or to support tactical or strategic changes.

Accounting - It is the way toward recording monetary exchanges relating to a business and set of accounting processes with integrated procedures and controls. The intent of an accounting is to record business transactions, summarize those transactions into an aggregated form, and create reports that can be used by decision makers to monitor, analyze, and improve operations.

Information - It is the Data that is gathered throughout the system and a collection of multiple pieces of equipment involved in the collection, processing, storage, and dissemination of information. Hardware, software, computer system connections and information, users.

Management - It is an interaction of preparation, dynamic, arranging, driving, inspiration and a set policies, processes and procedures used by an organization to ensure that it can fulfill the tasks required to achieve its objectives. These objectives cover many aspects of the organization’s operations (including financial success, safe operation, product quality, client relationships, legislative and regulatory conformance and worker management) the HR, monetary, physical, and data assets of an association to. arrive at its objectives proficiently and adequately.
